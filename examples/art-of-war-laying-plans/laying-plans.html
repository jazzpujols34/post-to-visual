The Art of War · Chapter I · 始計

Laying Plans

War, Sun Tzu opens, is a matter of life and death for the State — "a road either to safety or to ruin." So before the first move, you measure. The chapter is a checklist for that measuring: five things to weigh, seven questions to ask, and one blunt rule about how war is actually waged.

Sun Tzu（孫子）·tr. Lionel Giles, 1910 · public domain

In one line

Battles are won before they're fought — by the side that measured more carefully. Weigh the five factors, score the seven comparisons, and remember that all warfare is deception. Many calculations beforehand beat few.

01

The five constant factors (五事)

The art of war is governed by five things you take into account before any campaign. Know them and you win; ignore them and you fail.

道
The Moral Law
What puts the people in complete accord with their ruler — they will follow him into any danger. Call it shared purpose.
天
Heaven
Night and day, cold and heat, the seasons. The timing and conditions you don't control but must read.
地
Earth
Distances, danger and safety, open ground and narrow passes — the terrain and the chances of life and death on it.
將
The Commander
The leader's virtues: wisdom, sincerity, benevolence, courage, strictness.
法
Method & discipline
The system: organizing the army, ranks, supply roads, control of spending. The unglamorous logistics that decide outcomes.
02

How the deliberation runs

The five factors aren't a static list — they feed a process. Assess them, turn them into a side-by-side comparison, and out comes a forecast.

03

The seven considerations (七計)

Lay the two sides side by side and ask seven questions. "By means of these seven I can forecast victory or defeat."

  1. Which sovereign is imbued with the Moral Law?
  2. Which general has the most ability?
  3. With whom lie the advantages of Heaven and Earth?
  4. On which side is discipline more rigorously enforced?
  5. Which army is the stronger?
  6. On which side are officers and men more highly trained?
  7. In which army is there greater constancy in reward and punishment?
04

All warfare is based on deception

Having measured honestly, you then act dishonestly — toward the enemy. The chapter's most quoted line, and a short catalogue of how.

All warfare is based on deception.兵者，詭道也。

Seem unable
…when you are able to attack.
Seem inactive
…when your forces are in use.
Seem far away
…when you are near.
Seem near
…when you are far away.
Bait, then crush
Feign disorder to draw him in.
Irritate & flatter
Anger the hot-tempered; let the proud grow arrogant.

The chapter closes where it began — with arithmetic: "The general who wins makes many calculations in his temple before the battle; the general who loses makes but few." Whoever does the most counting beforehand can foresee the result.

05

名詞小抄

The five factors are usually cited by their single Chinese characters.

道
dào · the Moral Law
Shared purpose between ruler and people — the willingness to follow regardless of risk.
天
tiān · Heaven
Time and conditions: seasons, weather, day and night.
地
dì · Earth
Terrain: distance, safety vs danger, open vs narrow ground.
將
jiàng · the Commander
Wisdom, sincerity, benevolence, courage, strictness.
法
fǎ · Method & discipline
Organization, ranks, supply, and control of expenditure — the logistics.
計
jì · calculation
The "reckoning" in the temple — the deliberate weighing this whole chapter is about.
